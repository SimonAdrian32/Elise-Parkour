What is parkour?

Parkour is a sports discipline that focuses on getting from A to B as fast, elegant and smooth as possible. It mostly takes place in the city, where the users jump, climb, roll, swing, and many other things to get past obstacles. Parkour is an activity that can be practiced alone or with others, and is usually - but not exclusively - carried out in urban spaces. Parkour involves seeing one's environment in a new way, and imagining the possibilities for navigating it by movement around, across, through, over and under its features.

Parkour was developed in France, primarily by Raymond Belle, and further by his son David Belle and his group of friends during the late 1980s. The discipline was popularized in the late 1990s and 2000s through films, documentaries, and advertisements featuring David Belle and his friends.
